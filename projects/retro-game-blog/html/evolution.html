The Early Era
(1950s–1970s):

Began as academic experiments (OXO, 1952) and evolved into the first commercial arcade (Computer Space, 1971) and home console (Magnavox Odyssey, 1972), with Pong establishing the industry.

The Arcade & Home Console Boom
(1980s–1990s):

Iconic characters (Mario, Pac-Man) dominated, followed by the 16-bit console wars between Sega and Nintendo. The late 90s introduced 3D graphics and CD-based games via PlayStation.

The Online & Modern Era
(2000s–Present):

Online multiplayer became standard (Halo), and digital distribution (Steam, 2003) revolutionized access. Mobile games (iPhone, 2007) made gaming accessible to a massive, casual audience.

Modern Era & The Future
(Present-All Tomorrows):

Now there are ever expanding ways to make our own games online. We can build worlds within worlds that are only limited by our minds.
