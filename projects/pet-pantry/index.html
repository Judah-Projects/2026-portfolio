Pet Pantry

Welcome

Caring for a pet isn’t just about food — it’s about family. Across Canada, millions of people rely on the companionship, stability, and emotional support that animals provide, especially during difficult times. Yet as the cost of living continues to rise, many families are struggling to feed themselves and their beloved pets. In March 2025 alone, food banks across the country recorded nearly 2.2 million visits, the highest number ever documented, and double the usage seen in 2019.

For pet guardians facing financial hardship, this strain extends to their animals. A nationwide survey found that 70% of Canadians who own dogs or cats feed their pets before feeding themselves, underscoring just how deeply people prioritize their animals even when resources are scarce. Yet more than 80% of Canadians don’t know where to turn for pet food assistance, despite the existence of over 300 pet food support programs across the country.

Pet food banks play a crucial role in keeping families together. Without support, guardians may face heartbreaking decisions—skipping meals, going without essentials, or surrendering pets they love simply because they can’t afford to feed them. Programs like ours help prevent these outcomes by offering compassionate, judgment‑free assistance. In British Columbia alone, pet food bank initiatives have already helped thousands of families keep their pets at home, reducing shelter overcrowding and supporting community well‑being.

At Pet Pantry, we believe no animal should go hungry and no family should be separated because of financial hardship. Whether you’re here to seek support, offer help, or learn more, we’re grateful you’ve joined us in building a kinder, more connected community — one full belly at a time.

Mission

At Pet Pantry, our mission is simple: to keep pets and livestock with the families who love them. We provide accessible, compassionate food support so no guardian has to choose between meeting their own needs and feeding their animals. By reducing financial strain, preventing unnecessary surrenders, and strengthening rural and urban communities alike, we work to ensure every animal stays safe, nourished, and home where they belong.

Donations

Every bag of kibble, bale of hay, or dollar donated becomes a lifeline for families facing hardship. With food bank usage in Canada reaching record highs — nearly 2.2 million visits in March 2023, a 32% increase from the previous year and 78% higher than 2019 — the need for pet food support has never been greater. Your generosity helps prevent animals from going hungry and keeps them out of overcrowded shelters. When you give, you’re not just donating food — you’re preserving family bonds.

Volunteers

Volunteers are the hands and hearts behind every meal we share. From sorting donations to delivering food to remote communities, volunteers make it possible for us to support the growing number of Canadians seeking help. As more families turn to pet food banks — a trend reflected in the rise of community‑run programs across the country — your time and compassion directly strengthen the safety net that keeps animals fed and loved. When you volunteer, you become part of a movement rooted in kindness and community care.
